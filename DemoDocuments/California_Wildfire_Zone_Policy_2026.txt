CALIFORNIA REGIONAL UNDERWRITING BULLETIN
Bulletin No. CA-2026-07
Effective Date: March 1, 2026

SUBJECT: Enhanced Requirements for Properties in California Wildfire Hazard Zones

TO: All California Underwriters and Escrow Officers
FROM: California Regional Counsel
RE: Mandatory Wildfire Risk Assessment and Insurance Requirements

OVERVIEW:
Following the 2025 California legislative session, new requirements apply to all properties located in designated High or Very High Fire Hazard Severity Zones (FHSZ) as mapped by CAL FIRE.

AFFECTED AREAS:
This bulletin applies to properties in the following California counties with significant wildfire risk:
- Los Angeles County (Santa Monica Mountains, Malibu, Topanga Canyon areas)
- San Diego County (Ramona, Julian, Alpine, Descanso areas)
- Riverside County (San Jacinto Mountains, Idyllwild area)
- San Bernardino County (Big Bear, Lake Arrowhead, Running Springs areas)
- Ventura County (Ojai, Fillmore, Santa Paula areas)
- Santa Barbara County (Montecito, Santa Ynez Valley areas)
- Sonoma County (Fountaingrove, Mark West areas)
- Napa County (Atlas Peak, Howell Mountain areas)
- Shasta County (Redding, Anderson areas)
- Butte County (Paradise, Magalia, Chico foothills)

MANDATORY DOCUMENTATION:

1. WILDFIRE HAZARD ZONE VERIFICATION
   - Obtain CAL FIRE FHSZ map designation for property
   - Document zone classification: Moderate, High, or Very High
   - Include map reference in title commitment
   - Effective date: Must be current within 90 days

2. DEFENSIBLE SPACE COMPLIANCE CERTIFICATE
   Required for all properties in High or Very High FHSZ:
   - Certificate from licensed inspector confirming 100-foot defensible space
   - Compliance with California Public Resources Code Section 4291
   - Zone 0 (0-5 feet): No combustible materials
   - Zone 1 (5-30 feet): Reduced fuel load, maintained vegetation
   - Zone 2 (30-100 feet): Thinned trees, cleared brush
   - Certificate must be dated within 60 days of closing

3. FIRE INSURANCE VERIFICATION
   Minimum Coverage Requirements:
   - Dwelling coverage: 100% of replacement cost
   - Contents coverage: Minimum $100,000
   - Additional Living Expenses: Minimum 24 months
   - Deductible: Maximum 5% of dwelling coverage
   - Policy must include wildfire/brush fire coverage (not excluded)
   - Insurance company must be California-admitted carrier with A.M. Best rating of A- or better

4. HARDENING CERTIFICATION (for Very High FHSZ only)
   - Roof: Class A fire-rated materials
   - Vents: Ember-resistant or enclosed
   - Windows: Dual-pane or tempered glass
   - Siding: Non-combustible or ignition-resistant materials
   - Decks: Fire-resistant materials or treatment
   - Certification by licensed contractor or building inspector

UNDERWRITING REQUIREMENTS:

VERY HIGH FIRE HAZARD SEVERITY ZONE:
- Requires Regional Counsel approval for all transactions
- Mandatory site inspection by Stewart-approved inspector
- Fire insurance binder required at least 5 days before closing
- Defensible space certificate required
- Hardening certification required
- Premium surcharge: $500 for residential, $1,500 for commercial
- Special exception in Schedule B for wildfire risk

HIGH FIRE HAZARD SEVERITY ZONE:
- Requires Branch Manager approval
- Fire insurance binder required at least 3 days before closing
- Defensible space certificate required
- Hardening certification recommended but not required
- Premium surcharge: $250 for residential, $750 for commercial
- Standard exception in Schedule B for wildfire risk

MODERATE FIRE HAZARD SEVERITY ZONE:
- Standard underwriting approval
- Fire insurance verification required
- Defensible space certificate recommended
- No premium surcharge
- Standard Schedule B exceptions

TITLE COMMITMENT LANGUAGE:

For Very High FHSZ properties, include in Schedule B:
"The property is located in a Very High Fire Hazard Severity Zone as designated by CAL FIRE. Buyer acknowledges receipt of Natural Hazard Disclosure Statement and Fire Hazard Severity Zone disclosure. This policy does not insure against loss or damage caused by wildfire, and does not cover diminution in value due to wildfire risk or proximity to burned areas."

For High FHSZ properties, include in Schedule B:
"The property is located in a High Fire Hazard Severity Zone as designated by CAL FIRE. Buyer acknowledges receipt of Natural Hazard Disclosure Statement."

SPECIAL SITUATIONS:

POST-FIRE RECONSTRUCTION:
Properties rebuilt after wildfire damage require:
- Final building inspection approval
- Certificate of Occupancy
- Verification of compliance with current building codes
- Updated fire insurance reflecting new construction
- Soil stability report if on hillside
- Environmental clearance for debris removal
- 12-month seasoning period from completion of construction

VACANT LAND IN FHSZ:
- Requires disclosure of buildability restrictions
- Verify local ordinances regarding new construction in FHSZ
- Check for building moratoriums post-fire
- Confirm availability of fire insurance for future construction
- Document water supply for fire suppression

COMMERCIAL PROPERTIES:
- Requires fire suppression system inspection
- Sprinkler system certification
- Emergency evacuation plan on file
- Fire department access verification
- Minimum 20-foot fire break around structures

LENDER REQUIREMENTS:

Most California lenders now require for FHSZ properties:
- Escrow holdback for defensible space work (if not completed)
- Annual fire insurance renewal verification
- Tri-annual defensible space re-certification
- Impound account for fire insurance premiums
- Higher down payment (typically 20-25% minimum)

DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS:

Natural Hazard Disclosure Statement must include:
- CAL FIRE FHSZ map and property location
- Historical fire data for area (past 20 years)
- Evacuation route information
- Fire station response time
- Water supply for firefighting
- Cell phone coverage for emergency alerts

PREMIUM CALCULATION:

Base Premium + Wildfire Surcharge:
- Very High FHSZ: Base + $500 (residential) or Base + $1,500 (commercial)
- High FHSZ: Base + $250 (residential) or Base + $750 (commercial)
- Moderate FHSZ: Base premium only

Additional Endorsements Available:
- Post-Fire Reconstruction Coverage: +$350
- Diminished Value Protection: +$500
- Extended Wildfire Coverage: +$750

DECLINATION CRITERIA:

Stewart Title will DECLINE to insure if:
- Property in Very High FHSZ cannot obtain fire insurance
- Defensible space compliance cannot be achieved
- Property has active fire damage or is in evacuation zone
- Lender requires coverage we cannot provide
- Property has been red-tagged by fire marshal
- Access road does not meet fire department standards

APPROVAL AUTHORITY:

AUTOMATIC APPROVAL:
- Moderate FHSZ with standard fire insurance

BRANCH MANAGER APPROVAL:
- High FHSZ with all required documentation

REGIONAL COUNSEL APPROVAL:
- Very High FHSZ (all transactions)
- Any FHSZ property with fire damage history
- Properties in areas with active fire within 5 miles
- Transactions where fire insurance is difficult to obtain
