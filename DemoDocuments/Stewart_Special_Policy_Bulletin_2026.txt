STEWART TITLE GUARANTY COMPANY
SPECIAL POLICY BULLETIN NO. 2026-03
Effective Date: March 15, 2026

TO: All Underwriters and Title Officers
FROM: Chief Underwriting Counsel
RE: New Requirements for Solar Panel Installations on Insured Properties

BACKGROUND:
Due to the increasing prevalence of solar panel installations and associated financing arrangements, Stewart Title is implementing new underwriting guidelines effective immediately.

SOLAR PANEL OWNERSHIP MODELS:

1. OWNED OUTRIGHT BY PROPERTY OWNER
   - No special requirements
   - Solar panels are considered fixtures and part of the real property
   - Standard title insurance coverage applies

2. SOLAR LEASE AGREEMENTS
   - Requires UCC-1 Financing Statement search
   - Lease must be reviewed and approved by underwriting counsel
   - Exception required in Schedule B for the solar lease
   - Minimum lease term disclosure: Must be disclosed if lease extends beyond 20 years
   - Assignment provisions must allow transfer with property sale

3. POWER PURCHASE AGREEMENTS (PPA)
   - Similar requirements to solar leases
   - PPA must be assignable to subsequent property owners
   - Utility company must acknowledge the arrangement
   - Exception required for PPA in Schedule B

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION:

For Solar Leases and PPAs:
1. Copy of executed solar lease or PPA agreement
2. UCC-1 Financing Statement (if filed)
3. Subordination agreement from solar company (if mortgage is involved)
4. Proof of insurance naming property owner as additional insured
5. Maintenance agreement details
6. Removal and restoration bond (minimum $10,000)

UNDERWRITING CONSIDERATIONS:

HIGH RISK FACTORS:
- Solar lease term exceeds 25 years
- No subordination agreement from solar company
- Unclear removal/restoration obligations
- Solar company has filed UCC-1 without property owner consent
- Lease prohibits or restricts property sale

MEDIUM RISK FACTORS:
- Solar lease term 15-25 years
- Conditional subordination agreement
- Solar company requires buyer qualification for lease assumption
- Removal costs exceed $5,000

LOW RISK FACTORS:
- Solar lease term under 15 years
- Full subordination agreement in place
- Automatic lease transfer with property sale
- Property owner owns solar panels outright

TITLE COMMITMENT REQUIREMENTS:

Schedule B Exceptions must include:
"Solar panel installation and associated [lease/PPA] agreement dated [DATE] between [PROPERTY OWNER] and [SOLAR COMPANY], recorded as Instrument No. [NUMBER] in the Official Records of [COUNTY], affecting the property."

PREMIUM SURCHARGE:
- Solar lease or PPA with term over 20 years: Add $150 to base premium
- Solar lease or PPA with term under 20 years: Add $75 to base premium
- Owned solar panels: No surcharge

APPROVAL REQUIREMENTS:

REQUIRES REGIONAL COUNSEL APPROVAL:
- Any solar lease or PPA with term exceeding 25 years
- Solar company refuses to provide subordination agreement
- UCC-1 filing discovered without corresponding lease documentation
- Solar lease contains unusual or onerous terms

AUTOMATIC APPROVAL (with proper documentation):
- Solar panels owned outright by property owner
- Solar lease under 15 years with full subordination
- Standard PPA with automatic transfer provisions

SPECIAL SITUATIONS:

FORECLOSURE PROPERTIES:
- Verify solar lease/PPA status with solar company
- Determine if lease survived foreclosure
- Obtain estoppel certificate from solar company
- Consider title exception for potential solar company claims

COMMERCIAL PROPERTIES:
- Require environmental assessment if solar installation exceeds 50kW
- Verify zoning compliance for commercial solar installations
- Check for utility company interconnection agreements
- Review roof warranty implications
