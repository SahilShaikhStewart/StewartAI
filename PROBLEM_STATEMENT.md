# Problem Statement
## Stewart AI Platform — AI Ideathon 2026

---

## The Problem

Title insurance underwriting at Stewart involves processing thousands of documents daily — deeds, title commitments, liens, mortgages, surveys, and closing disclosures. Today this process is:

### 1. Slow and Manual
A title examiner receives a document and must:
- Read every page manually (15–30 minutes per document)
- Search through scattered policy bulletins to check requirements
- Manually extract key entities (parties, amounts, legal descriptions)
- Identify potential title defects by experience alone
- Enter data into systems by hand

At 100,000 documents per year, this is **50,000+ hours of manual labor annually**.

### 2. Knowledge is Locked Away
Stewart's underwriting policies, bulletins, and guidelines exist across SharePoint folders, email threads, and senior employees' institutional memory. A junior examiner asking *"What are our requirements for a property with solar panels?"* may spend 20 minutes searching — or simply not find the answer.

New hires take **6–12 months** to become proficient because there is no intelligent, searchable knowledge system.

### 3. Risk Assessment is Inconsistent
Two underwriters reviewing similar properties in the same state may reach different risk conclusions. There is no standardized, data-driven scoring system. Aggregating risk trends across states, property types, or transaction types requires manual reporting.

### 4. Handwritten and Scanned Documents Are a Bottleneck
Field agents photograph handwritten easement agreements, notarized documents, and property notes. These photos must be manually transcribed before review — adding 30+ minutes per document and introducing transcription errors.

### 5. No Audit Trail for AI-Assisted Decisions
As AI tools enter the workflow, regulators and compliance teams need to know: *What information was used? What was the basis for a risk decision?* Today there is no structured audit trail.

## The Impact

| Pain Point | Estimated Annual Cost |
|---|---|
| Manual document review (100K docs × 30 min × $50/hr) | **$2.5M** |
| Knowledge search time (100K queries × 20 min × $50/hr) | **$1.7M** |
| Inconsistent risk decisions (5% error rate on claims) | **$2.5M** |
| Manual transcription of handwritten/scanned docs | **$250K** |
| **Total** | **~$7M/year** |

---

## Who Is Affected

- **Title Examiners** — overwhelmed by volume, spending most time on repetitive extraction
- **Underwriters** — no standardized risk scoring, inconsistent decisions
- **Field Agents** — photos and handwritten notes create bottlenecks
- **Compliance Officers** — no audit trail for AI-assisted decisions
- **Customers** — delays in closing due to slow document processing

---

## The Opportunity

Modern multimodal AI (text + vision) combined with a domain-specific knowledge base can:
- Analyze a title document in **under 5 seconds**
- Answer policy questions instantly with **source citations**
- Provide **standardized risk scores** (0–100) with factor breakdown
- Process **photos and handwritten documents** without manual transcription
- Maintain a **complete audit trail** of every AI decision

This is not a generic AI chatbot. It is a **title insurance intelligence platform** built specifically for Stewart's workflows, policies, and data.
