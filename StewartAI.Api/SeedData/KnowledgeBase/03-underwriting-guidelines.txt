STEWART TITLE — UNDERWRITING GUIDELINES AND RISK ASSESSMENT
=============================================================

UNDERWRITING PRINCIPLES
Title insurance underwriting is the process of evaluating the risk associated with insuring a particular title. Stewart Title underwriters follow these core principles:

1. Marketability Standard: A title must be marketable — meaning a reasonable, prudent buyer would accept it without objection.
2. Insurability Standard: Even if a title has minor defects, it may still be insurable with appropriate exceptions or endorsements.
3. Risk-Based Pricing: Premium rates are set by state regulators, but the decision to insure is based on risk assessment.

RISK ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK

Stewart uses a multi-factor risk assessment model:

Factor 1: Geographic Risk
- High-risk states: Florida (fraud prevalence), California (complex regulations), New York (judicial foreclosure delays), Louisiana (Napoleonic code)
- Medium-risk states: Texas, Illinois, New Jersey, Nevada
- Lower-risk states: Minnesota (Torrens system available), Ohio, Pennsylvania, Virginia
- Factors: State foreclosure laws, recording systems, fraud rates, litigation environment

Factor 2: Transaction Type Risk
- Purchase (standard): LOW to MEDIUM risk
- Refinance: LOW risk (existing owner, known history)
- Cash Sale: MEDIUM risk (no lender oversight)
- Short Sale: HIGH risk (distressed seller, potential fraud)
- Foreclosure: HIGH risk (procedural defects, redemption rights)
- New Construction: MEDIUM risk (mechanic's lien exposure)
- REO (bank-owned): MEDIUM to HIGH risk (chain of title issues from foreclosure)

Factor 3: Property Type Risk
- Single Family Residential: LOW to MEDIUM
- Condominium: MEDIUM (HOA issues, common area complications)
- Multi-Family: MEDIUM to HIGH (more complex ownership, tenant rights)
- Commercial: HIGH (complex transactions, environmental issues)
- Vacant Land: HIGH (boundary disputes, access issues, zoning)
- Mixed Use: HIGH (multiple regulatory frameworks)

Factor 4: Transaction Value Risk
- Under $250,000: Standard underwriting
- $250,000 - $1,000,000: Enhanced review
- $1,000,000 - $5,000,000: Senior underwriter review required
- Over $5,000,000: Regional underwriting counsel approval required
- Over $20,000,000: National underwriting counsel approval required

Factor 5: Chain of Title Risk
- Clean chain (20+ years): LOW risk
- Recent transfers (within 3 years): MEDIUM risk — check for flipping fraud
- Multiple transfers in short period: HIGH risk — potential fraud indicator
- Estate/probate transfers: MEDIUM to HIGH risk
- Entity transfers (LLC, trust): MEDIUM risk — verify authority

UNDERWRITING DECISION MATRIX

APPROVE (Risk Score 0-39):
- Clean title search with no material defects
- All liens released or to be paid at closing
- Standard transaction type and property type
- No red flags in chain of title

APPROVE WITH CONDITIONS (Risk Score 40-69):
- Minor defects that can be cured before closing
- Standard exceptions required on Schedule B
- Additional documentation needed (affidavits, resolutions)
- Endorsements required for specific risks

REFER TO UNDERWRITER (Risk Score 70-89):
- Material defects requiring underwriter evaluation
- Complex transactions (commercial, multi-party)
- High-value transactions exceeding agent authority
- Unusual chain of title issues

DECLINE (Risk Score 90-100):
- Forged or void instruments in chain of title
- Active litigation affecting the property
- Unresolvable title defects
- Known fraud indicators
- Federal/state regulatory prohibitions

FRAUD RED FLAGS
Stewart underwriters are trained to identify these fraud indicators:
1. Property flipping: Multiple sales in short period with escalating prices
2. Straw buyers: Purchaser has no apparent connection to the property
3. Inflated appraisals: Purchase price significantly above market value
4. Identity theft: Seller's identity cannot be verified
5. Wire fraud: Last-minute changes to wiring instructions
6. Seller impersonation: Vacant land sales where seller is not the true owner
7. Forged documents: Signatures that don't match known samples
8. Power of attorney abuse: POA used for suspicious transactions

ENDORSEMENT GUIDELINES
Common ALTA endorsements and when to issue:

ALTA 8.1 (Environmental Protection Lien): Insures against environmental liens
ALTA 9 (Restrictions, Encroachments, Minerals): Comprehensive coverage for CC&Rs
ALTA 17 (Access and Entry): Insures legal access to the property
ALTA 19 (Contiguity): Insures that multiple parcels are contiguous
ALTA 22 (Location): Insures the property is located at a specific address
ALTA 25 (Same as Survey): Insures the legal description matches the survey
ALTA 28 (Encroachment): Insures against loss from existing encroachments
ALTA 35 (Minerals): Insures against loss from mineral rights claims

REINSURANCE REQUIREMENTS
- Single risk retention limit varies by state and policy type
- Excess risk must be reinsured through Stewart's reinsurance program
- Commercial transactions over $10 million typically require reinsurance
- Facultative reinsurance for unusual or high-risk transactions
