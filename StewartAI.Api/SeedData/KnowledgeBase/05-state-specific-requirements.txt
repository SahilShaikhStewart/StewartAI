STEWART TITLE — STATE-SPECIFIC TITLE INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================

Title insurance regulations vary significantly by state. This guide covers key differences for the most active real estate markets.

TEXAS (TX)
- Regulatory Body: Texas Department of Insurance (TDI)
- Rate Type: Promulgated rates — all companies must charge the same premium
- Attorney State: No — non-attorneys can conduct closings
- Community Property State: Yes — both spouses must sign conveyances of homestead
- Homestead Protection: Strong homestead protections under Texas Constitution
- Mechanic's Lien: Must be filed by 15th day of 4th month after work completed
- Transfer Tax: None
- Key Issues: Mineral rights are commonly severed from surface rights; always check for mineral reservations
- Stewart Guideline: Texas requires specific T-forms for title commitments and policies

CALIFORNIA (CA)
- Regulatory Body: California Department of Insurance
- Rate Type: File and use — companies set their own rates
- Attorney State: No — escrow companies handle closings
- Community Property State: Yes — both spouses must consent to transfers
- Preliminary Report: California uses "preliminary reports" instead of "commitments"
- Transfer Tax: County transfer tax of $1.10 per $1,000; some cities have additional taxes
- Mechanic's Lien: Must be filed within 90 days of completion for direct contractors
- Key Issues: Proposition 13 property tax implications; earthquake fault zones; environmental contamination
- Stewart Guideline: California requires specific CLTA/ALTA policy forms

FLORIDA (FL)
- Regulatory Body: Florida Office of Insurance Regulation
- Rate Type: Promulgated rates
- Attorney State: Varies by county — some require attorney closings
- Community Property State: No — but has tenancy by the entireties
- Homestead: Strong homestead protections — cannot be devised away from spouse
- Transfer Tax: Documentary stamp tax of $0.70 per $100 (except Miami-Dade: $0.60)
- Mechanic's Lien: Must be filed within 90 days of final furnishing
- Key Issues: High fraud rate; condo association issues; hurricane damage disclosure
- Stewart Guideline: Florida has the highest title insurance claim rate — enhanced due diligence required

NEW YORK (NY)
- Regulatory Body: New York Department of Financial Services
- Rate Type: Promulgated rates
- Attorney State: Yes — attorneys must conduct closings
- Community Property State: No — but has dower/curtesy rights
- Transfer Tax: State transfer tax of $2 per $500 for residential; NYC has additional taxes
- Mansion Tax: 1% surcharge on residential properties over $1 million
- Mortgage Recording Tax: Varies by county (NYC: 1.8% for loans under $500K, 1.925% for over)
- Key Issues: Cooperative apartments (co-ops) are not real property — different insurance requirements
- Stewart Guideline: New York requires specific rate manual compliance; co-op transactions need special handling

ILLINOIS (IL)
- Regulatory Body: Illinois Department of Insurance
- Rate Type: File and use
- Attorney State: Varies — Chicago area typically uses attorneys
- Transfer Tax: State tax of $0.50 per $500; Cook County has additional taxes
- Key Issues: Chicago has unique customs (seller typically pays for owner's policy)
- Stewart Guideline: Illinois has specific requirements for trust deed states

PENNSYLVANIA (PA)
- Regulatory Body: Pennsylvania Insurance Department
- Rate Type: Promulgated rates
- Attorney State: No — title companies handle closings
- Transfer Tax: 1% state tax plus 1% local tax (2% total in most areas; Philadelphia is 4.278%)
- Key Issues: Coal rights and mineral rights in western PA
- Stewart Guideline: Pennsylvania requires specific rate compliance and has unique reissue rate rules

GEORGIA (GA)
- Regulatory Body: Georgia Office of Insurance and Safety Fire Commissioner
- Rate Type: File and use
- Attorney State: Yes — attorneys must conduct closings
- Transfer Tax: State transfer tax of $1 per $1,000
- Key Issues: Security deeds (not mortgages); non-judicial foreclosure state
- Stewart Guideline: Georgia uses security deeds instead of mortgages — different recording requirements

OHIO (OH)
- Regulatory Body: Ohio Department of Insurance
- Rate Type: File and use
- Attorney State: No
- Transfer Tax: Conveyance fee of $1 per $1,000 (county) plus possible municipal fees
- Key Issues: Dower rights must be released; survivorship deeds common
- Stewart Guideline: Ohio has specific dower release requirements that must be verified

GENERAL STATE CATEGORIES

Attorney States (closing must involve an attorney):
Connecticut, Delaware, Georgia, Massachusetts, New York, North Carolina, South Carolina, Vermont, Virginia, West Virginia

Non-Attorney States (title companies can conduct closings):
Arizona, California, Colorado, Florida (varies), Idaho, Minnesota, Montana, Nevada, New Mexico, Oregon, Texas, Utah, Washington

Community Property States:
Arizona, California, Idaho, Louisiana, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas, Washington, Wisconsin

Judicial Foreclosure States (foreclosure requires court action):
Connecticut, Delaware, Florida, Hawaii, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, Michigan, Minnesota, Montana, Nebraska, New Jersey, New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, South Dakota, Vermont, Virginia, Wisconsin

Non-Judicial Foreclosure States (foreclosure without court):
Alabama, Alaska, Arizona, Arkansas, California, Colorado, Georgia, Idaho, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, Nevada, New Hampshire, Oregon, Rhode Island, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Virginia, Washington, West Virginia, Wyoming
