STEWART TITLE — REAL ESTATE CLOSING PROCESS AND ESCROW
========================================================

THE CLOSING PROCESS OVERVIEW
A real estate closing (also called settlement) is the final step in a property transaction where ownership is transferred from seller to buyer. Stewart Title facilitates closings through its direct operations and agent network.

STEP-BY-STEP CLOSING PROCESS

Step 1: Opening the Title Order
- Buyer, seller, or their agents submit a title order to Stewart
- Required information: property address, legal description, buyer/seller names, lender information, purchase price
- Stewart assigns a file number and begins the title search

Step 2: Title Search and Examination
- Title examiner searches public records for the property
- Typical search period: 20-60 years depending on state requirements
- Records searched: deeds, mortgages, liens, judgments, taxes, plats, easements
- Examiner prepares a title abstract or title report

Step 3: Title Commitment Issuance
- Stewart issues a title commitment (preliminary title report)
- Schedule A: Identifies the property, proposed insured parties, and coverage amounts
- Schedule B-I: Lists requirements to be satisfied before closing (payoffs, releases, documents needed)
- Schedule B-II: Lists exceptions that will appear on the final policy
- Commitment is sent to all parties for review

Step 4: Curative Work
- Any title defects identified must be resolved
- Common curative items: obtaining lien releases, recording missing documents, correcting legal descriptions
- Stewart's curative team works with parties to clear requirements
- All Schedule B-I requirements must be satisfied before closing

Step 5: Closing Preparation
- Closing documents are prepared (deed, mortgage, settlement statement)
- HUD-1 Settlement Statement or TRID Closing Disclosure is prepared
- Wire instructions are sent to parties (with fraud prevention protocols)
- Closing date, time, and location are confirmed

Step 6: The Closing
- All parties sign required documents
- Buyer signs: mortgage/deed of trust, promissory note, closing disclosure
- Seller signs: deed, seller's affidavit, transfer tax declarations
- Notarization of signatures as required by state law
- Funds are collected (cashier's check or wire transfer)

Step 7: Recording
- Signed documents are submitted to the county recorder's office
- Deed is recorded to transfer ownership
- Mortgage/deed of trust is recorded to secure the lender's interest
- Recording fees are paid from closing funds

Step 8: Disbursement
- After recording confirmation, escrow funds are disbursed
- Seller receives net proceeds
- Existing liens are paid off
- Real estate commissions are paid
- Title insurance premiums, recording fees, and taxes are paid
- Any remaining funds are returned to the appropriate party

Step 9: Policy Issuance
- After recording, Stewart issues the final title insurance policies
- Owner's policy is sent to the buyer
- Lender's policy is sent to the mortgage lender
- Policies are effective as of the recording date and time

ESCROW SERVICES

What is Escrow?
Escrow is a neutral third-party arrangement where funds and documents are held until all conditions of the transaction are met. Stewart Title serves as the escrow agent in many transactions.

Escrow Agent Responsibilities:
1. Hold earnest money deposits in a trust account
2. Collect and disburse funds according to closing instructions
3. Ensure all conditions are met before disbursement
4. Prepare settlement statements
5. Record documents with the county
6. Issue title insurance policies
7. Report transactions to the IRS (Form 1099-S)

Escrow Account Requirements:
- Funds must be held in FDIC-insured accounts
- Separate trust accounts from operating accounts
- Regular reconciliation of escrow accounts
- Compliance with state escrow regulations
- Interest-bearing accounts where required by state law

TRID (TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure) COMPLIANCE
- Loan Estimate must be provided within 3 business days of loan application
- Closing Disclosure must be provided at least 3 business days before closing
- Changes to certain terms require a new 3-day waiting period
- Stewart's closing teams are trained on TRID compliance requirements

WIRE FRAUD PREVENTION
Stewart Title takes wire fraud prevention seriously:
1. Never change wire instructions based on email alone
2. Always verify wire instructions by phone using a known number
3. Use Stewart's secure wire verification system
4. Report suspicious communications immediately
5. Educate buyers about wire fraud risks before closing
6. Use encrypted email for sensitive financial information

REMOTE/DIGITAL CLOSINGS
Stewart supports modern closing methods:
- Remote Online Notarization (RON): Available in states that permit it
- Hybrid closings: Some documents signed remotely, some in person
- eClosings: Fully electronic document signing and notarization
- Mobile closings: Notary travels to the signer's location

CLOSING COSTS BREAKDOWN
Typical closing costs include:
- Title insurance premiums (owner's and lender's policies)
- Title search and examination fees
- Escrow/closing fees
- Recording fees
- Transfer taxes (vary by state and locality)
- Survey fees
- Attorney fees (required in some states)
- Lender fees (origination, appraisal, credit report)
- Prepaid items (insurance, taxes, interest)
- HOA transfer fees and estoppel letters
