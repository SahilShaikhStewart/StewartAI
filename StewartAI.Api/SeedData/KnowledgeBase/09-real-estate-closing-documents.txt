REAL ESTATE CLOSING DOCUMENTS — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
Stewart Title — Document Intelligence Reference
=================================================

PROMISSORY NOTE
What it is: The borrower's promise to repay the loan.
Key Elements:
- Principal amount: Total amount being borrowed
- Interest rate: Annual percentage rate (APR)
- Payment schedule: Monthly payment amount and due date
- Term length: Repayment period (e.g., 15 or 30 years)
- Late payment fees: Penalties for missed or late payments
- Prepayment terms: Whether early payoff is allowed without penalty
- Default terms: What happens if payments are missed
Risk Factors: Missing signature pages, blurred financial amounts, incomplete borrower information
Stewart Guideline: All pages required, clear signature lines, all financial figures must be legible

DEED OF TRUST / MORTGAGE
What it is: Legal document securing the loan with the property as collateral.
Key Elements:
- Property legal description: Complete and accurate legal description
- Lender information: Who holds the mortgage
- All parties listed: Grantors, grantees, trustees
- Borrower obligations: Responsibilities as homeowner
- Default terms: Consequences of non-payment
- Insurance requirements: Required property insurance coverage
- Notarization blocks: Clearly marked for notary use
- Recording information: Spaces for official county recording
Risk Factors: Incomplete legal descriptions, missing party names, improper notarization blocks
Stewart Guideline: Complete deed with all exhibits, property legal description attachments, state-specific requirements

CLOSING DISCLOSURE (CD)
What it is: Summary of all final loan terms and closing costs (replaced HUD-1 under TRID).
Key Elements:
- Final loan amount: Confirmed loan principal
- Monthly payment: Principal, interest, taxes, insurance (PITI)
- All closing costs: Every fee itemized
- Cash to close: Exact amount needed at closing
- Rate lock: Interest rate and expiration date
- Comparison: Shows changes from original Loan Estimate
Legal Requirements:
- Must be provided 3 business days before closing (TRID requirement)
- Material changes trigger new CD and new 3-day waiting period
- Cannot be modified without restarting the waiting period
- Borrower acknowledges receipt and understanding
Stewart Guideline: Final version only (no drafts), all pages included (typically 3-5 pages), verify borrower information matches loan application

WARRANTY DEED
What it is: Legal document transferring property ownership with full title guarantees.
Key Elements:
- Grantor: Person/entity selling the property
- Grantee: Person/entity buying the property
- Property description: Legal description meeting local recording standards
- Consideration: Purchase price or "valuable consideration"
- Title warranties: Seller guarantees clear title
- Notarization: Required for recording at county courthouse
Comparison with Quitclaim Deed:
- Warranty Deed: Full protection, seller guarantees clear title, title insurance usually accompanies, seller liable if title problems arise
- Quitclaim Deed: No warranties, "as-is" transfer, buyer assumes all title risks, common in family transfers and divorces

AFFIDAVITS AND SWORN STATEMENTS
Common Types in Real Estate:
- Occupancy Affidavit: Statement about who will live in the property
- Name Affidavit: Confirmation of legal name (resolves name discrepancies)
- Title Affidavit: Statement about property ownership history
- Property Condition Disclosure: Known issues with the property
Requirements: Sworn statement format, specific facts, jurat format requiring oath/affirmation, no blanks in sworn content

POWER OF ATTORNEY (POA)
Special Requirements for Real Estate:
- Must be current and valid (not revoked or expired)
- Must specifically cover real estate transactions
- Principal must be competent to grant authority
- May require additional witnesses beyond notarization
- Some POAs must be recorded to be effective
Signing Format: "John Smith by Mary Smith as Attorney-in-Fact"
Stewart Guideline: Original POA should accompany signing, verify POA validity before proceeding

DOCUMENTS BY TRANSACTION TYPE

Purchase Transactions Required:
- Promissory Note
- Deed of Trust/Mortgage
- Warranty Deed
- Closing Disclosure
- Property Transfer Affidavit
- Title Insurance Policy
Additional: Power of Attorney (if applicable), Corporate Resolution (business entities), Occupancy Affidavit, Property Disclosure Forms, HOA documents, Survey or Plat

Refinance Transactions Required:
- Promissory Note (new)
- Deed of Trust/Mortgage (new)
- Closing Disclosure
- Right to Cancel (3-day rescission notice)
- Occupancy Affidavit
Additional: Rate Lock Agreement, Property Insurance documentation, Tax and Insurance Escrow agreements

HELOC/Second Mortgage Required:
- HELOC Agreement or Second Mortgage Note
- Deed of Trust (subordinate lien)
- Truth in Lending Disclosure
- Right to Cancel (3-day notice)
- Property Appraisal (if required)

Cash Purchase Required:
- Warranty Deed or Quitclaim Deed
- Settlement Statement
- Property Transfer Affidavit
- Title Insurance Policy

RIGHT TO CANCEL (RESCISSION PERIOD)
Applies to: Refinances, HELOCs, home equity loans
Duration: 3 business days after signing
Rules: Business days only (excludes Saturdays, Sundays, holidays), must cancel by midnight on 3rd business day, can cancel for any reason
Impact: Loan doesn't fund until 4th business day, plan accordingly, ensure rate lock covers cancellation period

DOCUMENT QUALITY STANDARDS
File Formats: PDF (preferred), TIFF, PNG, JPG/JPEG
Resolution: Minimum 200 DPI, recommended 300 DPI
File Size: Maximum 50MB per document, recommended 10MB or less
Naming Convention: [DocumentType]_[PropertyAddress]_[Date].pdf
Quality Checklist: All text readable, pages straight and properly oriented, no cut-off content, consistent lighting, no shadows or glare

COMMON DOCUMENT ISSUES AND RESOLUTION
- Missing pages: Verify page count matches expected total, contact document provider
- Blurred text: Rescan at higher resolution (300 DPI), clean scanner glass
- Inconsistent information: Ensure names match exactly across all documents
- Wrong amounts: Stop signing, verify changes, request corrected documents
- Missing signature lines: Contact document preparer for revision
- Incompatible formats: Convert to PDF format before upload
