COMPLIANCE AND REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
Stewart Title — Legal and Regulatory Reference
================================================

FEDERAL REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

E-SIGN Act (Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act)
- Federal law enacted in 2000 establishing legal validity of electronic signatures
- Electronic signatures have same legal standing as handwritten signatures
- Applies to interstate and foreign commerce transactions
- Consumer consent required before using electronic records
- Records must be accessible and retainable by consumers

UETA (Uniform Electronic Transactions Act)
- Model state law adopted by 49 states (all except New York, which has similar legislation)
- Provides legal framework for electronic transactions at state level
- Electronic records and signatures satisfy legal requirements for written documents
- Establishes rules for attribution, effect, and retention of electronic records

TRID (TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure)
- Combined Truth in Lending Act (TILA) and Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (RESPA)
- Requires Loan Estimate within 3 business days of loan application
- Requires Closing Disclosure at least 3 business days before closing
- Material changes to CD trigger new 3-day waiting period
- Applies to most closed-end consumer mortgage loans
- Penalties for non-compliance include fines and potential loan rescission

RESPA (Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act)
- Prohibits kickbacks and referral fees in real estate settlement services
- Requires disclosure of settlement costs to borrowers
- Limits escrow account deposits
- Requires servicer to respond to borrower inquiries
- Stewart compliance: All settlement services disclosed, no undisclosed referral arrangements

Truth in Lending Act (TILA)
- Requires disclosure of APR, finance charges, and total payments
- Provides right of rescission for certain transactions (refinances, HELOCs)
- 3-day cooling-off period for applicable transactions
- Advertising requirements for credit terms

Fair Housing Act
- Prohibits discrimination in housing-related transactions
- Protected classes: Race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, disability
- Applies to lending, insurance, and real estate services
- Stewart policy: Zero tolerance for discriminatory practices

STATE-SPECIFIC REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS

Notarization Laws:
- Each state has specific notary public laws and regulations
- RON authorized in 40+ states with varying requirements
- Some states require specific notarial certificate language
- Witness requirements vary by state (some require additional witnesses)
- Recording office requirements differ by jurisdiction

Attorney State Requirements:
States requiring attorney involvement in real estate closings:
- Connecticut, Delaware, Georgia, Massachusetts, New York, North Carolina, South Carolina, Vermont, West Virginia
- Some states require attorney review but not presence
- Stewart guideline: Always verify attorney requirements for the specific state and transaction type

Community Property States:
- Arizona, California, Idaho, Louisiana, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas, Washington, Wisconsin
- Both spouses may need to sign even if only one is on the loan
- Special considerations for property acquired during marriage
- Stewart guideline: Verify spousal consent requirements in community property states

MORTGAGE INDUSTRY STANDARDS

MISMO (Mortgage Industry Standards Maintenance Organization)
- Establishes technology standards for the mortgage industry
- RON technology standards for electronic notarization
- eNote standards for electronic promissory notes
- Data exchange standards between lenders, title companies, and servicers
- Stewart compliance: MISMO certified RON adherence

GSE Requirements (Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac):
- Specific requirements for loans sold to government-sponsored enterprises
- eNote acceptance criteria and registration requirements
- MERS (Mortgage Electronic Registration Systems) requirements
- Quality control standards for loan documentation
- Stewart guideline: Verify GSE requirements for each transaction

FHA/VA/USDA Loan Requirements:
- Government-backed loans have additional documentation requirements
- FHA: Federal Housing Administration specific forms and disclosures
- VA: Veterans Affairs loan guaranty requirements
- USDA: Rural development loan specific requirements
- Each program has unique closing document requirements

TITLE INSURANCE REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

State Insurance Department Oversight:
- Title insurance rates regulated by state insurance departments
- Filing requirements for rate changes and policy forms
- Consumer protection regulations specific to title insurance
- Complaint handling procedures mandated by regulators

ALTA (American Land Title Association) Standards:
- Industry-wide standards for title insurance policies
- Best Practices Framework (7 pillars):
  1. Licensing: Maintain appropriate licenses
  2. Escrow Trust Accounting: Proper handling of client funds
  3. Privacy and Information Security: Protect consumer data
  4. Settlement Procedures: Follow proper closing procedures
  5. Title Policy Production: Accurate and timely policy issuance
  6. Professional Liability Insurance: Maintain adequate coverage
  7. Consumer Complaints: Proper complaint handling procedures

ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) AND FRAUD PREVENTION

Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) Compliance:
- Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements
- Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) for unusual transactions
- Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs) for cash transactions over $10,000
- Record keeping requirements for financial transactions

FinCEN Geographic Targeting Orders (GTOs):
- Special reporting requirements for all-cash real estate purchases
- Applies to specific geographic areas and transaction thresholds
- Title companies must identify beneficial owners of shell companies
- Stewart compliance: Automated screening and reporting procedures

Wire Fraud Prevention:
- Verification procedures for wire transfer instructions
- Multi-factor authentication for wire requests
- Callback verification to known phone numbers
- Employee training on social engineering and phishing
- Stewart protocol: Never change wire instructions via email alone

PRIVACY AND DATA PROTECTION

Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA):
- Requires financial institutions to protect consumer financial information
- Privacy notices to consumers about information sharing practices
- Safeguards Rule: Implement security program for customer information
- Stewart compliance: Comprehensive information security program

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):
- Consumer rights: Know what data is collected, delete data, opt out of sale
- Applies to businesses meeting certain thresholds
- Penalties for non-compliance
- Stewart compliance: Privacy policy, consumer request procedures

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation):
- Applies to processing of EU residents' personal data
- Data subject rights: Access, rectification, erasure, portability
- Data protection impact assessments for high-risk processing
- Stewart compliance: Where applicable for international transactions

AUDIT AND RECORD KEEPING REQUIREMENTS

Session Recording Retention:
- RON session recordings: Minimum 10 years (varies by state)
- Notary journals: Per state requirements (typically 3-10 years)
- Document copies: As legally required for audit trail
- Personal information: Only as long as legally required
- Payment information: Per PCI compliance requirements

Audit Trail Requirements:
- Complete documentation of all notarization sessions
- Identity verification records (ID examination, KBA results)
- Document integrity verification (hash values, tamper evidence)
- System access and activity logs
- Chain of custody documentation for legal proceedings

INSURANCE AND LIABILITY

Notary Bonds: All notaries bonded as required by state law
Errors and Omissions (E&O): Professional liability insurance for notaries
Technology Insurance: Platform and system integrity coverage
Cyber Liability: Data breach and privacy violation coverage
General Liability: Comprehensive business liability protection

Dispute Resolution Process:
1. Documentation review: Examine session recordings and records
2. Notary consultation: Interview the performing notary
3. Investigation: Thorough review of all evidence
4. Resolution: Work toward satisfactory resolution
5. Insurance: E&O coverage if applicable
6. Legal cooperation: Assist with any legal proceedings
